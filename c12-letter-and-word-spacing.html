Breed History

The Briard, which is belived to have originated in france, has been bred for centuries to beed and to protect sheep. The breed was used by the French Army as sentries, messengers and to serach for wounded soldiers because of its fine sense of hearing. Briards were used in the First Worl War almost to the point of extinetion. Currently the population of Briards is slowly recovering. Charlemage, Napoleon, Thomas Jefferson and Lafayestte all owned Briards.
